Many CRM projects focus on the software. But success depends on three components that need to be in balance.

## 1. The behaviour of you and your team

The success of a CRM begins with the human factor: the behaviour of you and your team. Focus on building lasting customer relationships rather than just selling. Listen to customers, show empathy and act proactively. Invest in training and coaching to optimise interactions.

## 2. Processes

Well-defined processes streamline the work and reduce errors. They ensure consistency in the customer experience across all touchpoints. CRM software such as Salesforce makes it possible to measure and optimise processes, and with regular feedback you adapt them to changing needs.

## 3. Technological support

A CRM system is the central gathering point for customer data. It automates tasks, analyses performance and ensures that staff have the right information at the right moment — which improves the customer experience and effectiveness.

## In balance

These three work as a single whole: behaviour forms the basis for customer relationships, processes ensure efficiency and consistency, and technology supports and optimises both.
