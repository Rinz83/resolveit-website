E-invoicing via Peppol is becoming the norm throughout Europe. In Belgium, an obligation applies from **1 January 2026** for all B2B invoices. Are you ready for it?

## What the law requires

- **Mandatory** for all B2B invoices in Belgium.
- **Format**: UBL/XML in accordance with the European standard EN 16931.
- **Transmission**: via the Peppol network.
- **No longer valid**: a PDF by email no longer counts as an official invoice.
- **Exception**: B2C transactions fall outside the obligation for now.

## Why the government is introducing this

The Belgian government wants to reduce administrative burdens and manual processing, speed up payments through automated systems, and curb fraud and VAT gaps through standardisation.

## What this means for your organisation

Your accounting software must support Peppol: WinBooks users may have to migrate. Data accuracy (VAT numbers, bank details, references) becomes crucial, and your entire order-to-cash process calls for redesign. That affects sales, finance and administration alike.
