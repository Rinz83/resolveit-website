A perfectly configured system has no value if no one uses it. Adoption is therefore just as important as the technology.

## Communicate the why

Share why a CRM is needed, what the added value for the end user is, how Salesforce works and what day-to-day work will look like. Understanding beforehand prevents resistance afterwards.

## Management leads by example

Managers and leaders must set a good example by actively using Salesforce themselves. Use Salesforce data in meetings to underpin the message and demonstrate its practical application. Make sure leaders are well trained in advance.

## Invest in key users

Identify enthusiastic Salesforce users who can guide colleagues and share their positive experience. When capable staff convey their enthusiasm, they encourage broader adoption across the entire organisation.

## Keep improving

Evaluate Salesforce regularly after implementation, so that it continues to align with changing needs and goals. Schedule periodic sessions with key users and CRM specialists to make the most of the platform's value.
