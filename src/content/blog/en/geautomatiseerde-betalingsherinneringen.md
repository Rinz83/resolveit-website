Not every customer pays in the same way. One pays immediately, another has internal approval processes, and some simply need a nudge. Good accounts receivable management takes those differences into account, and that is exactly where the Billing Platform's automated reminder system excels.

## A multi-stage approach

Instead of a single standard demand letter, the system works with several stages, each with an appropriate tone. The first reminder is friendly and informal: often an invoice has simply been overlooked. If payment still does not arrive, a more direct second reminder follows, and if necessary a final, formal warning. This keeps the relationship in good shape, even when a payment takes a while to come through.

## Adaptable by customer, country and language

The reminders adapt to the type of customer, the country and the language settings. An international customer receives their reminder in the right language and tone, which prevents unnecessary irritation and misunderstandings.

## Smart about timing

The system takes public holidays, weekends and time zones into account. As a result, reminders arrive at a logical and appropriate moment, not in the middle of the night or on a public holiday, which benefits professionalism.

## Everything logged in Salesforce

Every reminder sent is logged against the customer in Salesforce. As a result, your team always has a complete picture of the communication history: who received what and when. No more stray emails that nobody can find.

## Less manual work, more focus on the relationship

Because the routine follow-up runs automatically, your finance team no longer has to spend time on standard chasing and emailing. That time is freed up for the cases that do require personal attention. Automation does not replace empathy: it actually enables more targeted relationship management.
