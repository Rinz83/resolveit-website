A Salesforce implementation is more than just rolling out software. It is a strategic journey that touches your processes, your people and your customers. In this article we share the checklist we use at Resolve IT.

## 1. Start with strategy, not with technology

Before a single field is created, we map out your business goals. What do you want to achieve with Salesforce? Which processes do you want to improve? Without clear goals, every implementation becomes a collection of disconnected features.

## 2. Involve your team early

The best implementation fails if no one uses the system. Involve future users from the very beginning, so that the system aligns with their daily work.

## 3. Keep it simple at the start

Begin with a working foundation and build out from there. A system that is too complex at launch causes confusion and resistance.

## 4. Invest in data migration

Poor data leads to poor decisions. Clean up your data before the migration and validate afterwards.

## 5. Train, train, train

User training is not an afterthought but an investment. Well-trained users get more value out of Salesforce and make fewer mistakes.
